Exponential Integral for Radiometric Altitude Corrections Reviewed

Adjusting counts of airborne gamma-ray spectrometric surveys to a consistent altitude is standard operating proceedure in exploration geophysics. It is quite common to use an exponential function to model the decay of gamma-quanta in the atmosphere. The relationship between gamma quanta counts and altitude AGL is N = N_0 e^{-\mu x}, where N_0 is the quanta count expected at ground level, N is the quanta count expected at an elevation x, and \mu is the linear attenutation coefficient of air.

Standard Altitude Correction N = N_0 e^{-\mu x}

By this statement, higher one goes (x increases), the less counts N will be measured. This is true in the near surface envionment, where the gamma quanta density is to first order a function of the near surface environment. But at higher altitudes, where radiation is primarily of cosmic origin, the opposite will be true. The cosmic background will increase with elevation x, and the standard altitude correction will be a poor model. But in exploration geophysics, the standard altitude correction model is fantastic.
